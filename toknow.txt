Google provides the conversion tools. Our application automates the decision-making around them.

Think of it like this:

Google gives:

LiteRT converters
    ↓
"Here are APIs to convert models"

But the user still needs to know:

Which converter should I use? (LiteRT-Torch, LiteRT-LM, TensorFlow)
Is my model supported?
What architecture is this?
What quantization should I choose?
Should I use CPU/GPU/NPU delegate?
How do I validate the converted model?
Did conversion reduce performance or accuracy?
How do I repeat this process for hundreds of models?

Our application provides:

Any HuggingFace model
        ↓
Automatic inspection
        ↓
Backend selection
        ↓
Optimization strategy
        ↓
Conversion
        ↓
Validation
        ↓
Deployment-ready LiteRT model

The analogy:

PyTorch provides torch.compile(), but tools like Hugging Face Optimum automate optimization workflows.
Docker provides container technology, but Kubernetes manages deployment decisions.
LLVM provides compilers, but build systems automate the process.

Our application is an automation and intelligence layer on top of LiteRT, not a replacement for LiteRT.

The value is especially when someone has:

thousands of Hugging Face models
different hardware targets
non-expert users who don't know LiteRT internals

They don't want to learn every converter API. They want:

"Give me my model, give me the optimized version for my device."

That's the problem we are solving.
